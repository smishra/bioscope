Gene
Sequences of length 5-20 bases (exist on either side of the gene)  control the
gene transcription. Such sequences often are over represented near the gene they
regulate.  They co-ordinate in controlling the gene transcription. It is believed
such short motifs are highly preserved due to their functionality and are transferred
across organisms with minor changes.
